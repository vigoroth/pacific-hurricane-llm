<p>The <b>1975 Pacific hurricane season</b> officially started May 15, 1975, in the eastern Pacific, and June 1, 1975, in the central Pacific, and lasted until November 30, 1975. These dates conventionally delimit the period of each year when most <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclones</a> form in the northeast <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-season_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-season-1"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The 1975 Pacific hurricane season was slightly above average, with 17 tropical storms forming. Of these, 9 became hurricanes, and 4 became major hurricanes by reaching Category 3 or higher on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>. The only notable storms are <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Olivia_(1975)" title="Hurricane Olivia (1975)">Hurricane Olivia</a>, which killed 30 people, caused $30 million (1975 USD) in damage, and left thousands homeless when it made landfall in October; and an unnamed hurricane that developed at very high latitude, but had no effect on land. Hurricane Denise was the strongest storm of the year. Hurricanes Lily and Katrina passed close to <a href="/wiki/Socorro_Island" title="Socorro Island">Socorro Island</a> and Tropical Storm Eleanor made landfall in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>. Hurricane Agatha sank a ship.
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Season_summary">Season summary</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Season summary"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="center" style="width:auto; margin-left:auto; margin-right:auto;">
<div class="timeline-wrapper"><map name="timeline_6xi1r25hwnwz00aso5hh213i31iwpyk"><area alt="Hurricane Olivia (1975)" coords="615,9,712,30" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Olivia_(1975)" shape="rect" title="Hurricane Olivia (1975)"/><area alt="Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale" coords="602,160,815,180" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" shape="rect" title="Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale"/></map><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/timeline/6xi1r25hwnwz00aso5hh213i31iwpyk.png" usemap="#timeline_6xi1r25hwnwz00aso5hh213i31iwpyk"/></div>
</div>
<p>The season began with the formation of Tropical Depression One on June 2 and ended with the extratropical transition of Tropical Storm Priscilla on November 7. No named systems formed in May, two in June, four in July, six in August, three in September, one in October, and one in November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The total length of the season, from the formation of the first depression to the dissipation of the last, was 158 days.
</p><p>In the eastern Pacific (east of <a href="/wiki/140th_meridian_west" title="140th meridian west">140°W</a>), sixteen tropical storms and eight hurricanes formed. Four of the hurricanes became major hurricanes by reaching Category 3 or higher on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> These numbers are close to the long-term averages of fifteen tropical storms, nine hurricanes, and four major hurricanes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-climatology_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-climatology-3"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> There were also four unnamed tropical depressions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The only system to enter or form in the central Pacific (between 140°W and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_dateline" title="International dateline">international dateline</a>) was an unusual high-latitude hurricane (north of <a href="/wiki/30th_parallel_north" title="30th parallel north">30°N</a>), which went unnamed. This was the first occurrence on record of a mid-Pacific upper tropospheric low developing into a tropical cyclone, and the first hurricane on record within the central Pacific north of Hawaii.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC1975_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-CPHC1975-5"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The only systems to make landfall this year were Tropical Storm Eleanor and Hurricane Olivia. Besides these two systems, Hurricane Agatha caused the sinking of a ship carrying millions of dollars worth of cargo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stricken_ship_in_tow_sinks_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-stricken_ship_in_tow_sinks-6"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disabled_freighter_sinks_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-disabled_freighter_sinks-7"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Tropical Storm Eleanor caused no serious impact.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The most devastating storm of the season was Hurricane Olivia. A late-season major hurricane, Olivia <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">came ashore</a> near <a href="/wiki/Mazatl%C3%A1n" title="Mazatlán">Mazatlán</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The storm killed 30 people, left around 30,000 people homeless,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday-8"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> and caused 20 million dollars (1975 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Dollar" title="United States Dollar">USD</a>) in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Systems">Systems</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Systems"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Hurricane_Agatha">Hurricane Agatha</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hurricane Agatha"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #FFFFD9">Category 1 hurricane (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:AgathaJun419751745UTCSMS2.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="975" data-file-width="1240" decoding="async" height="118" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/AgathaJun419751745UTCSMS2.png/150px-AgathaJun419751745UTCSMS2.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/AgathaJun419751745UTCSMS2.png/225px-AgathaJun419751745UTCSMS2.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/AgathaJun419751745UTCSMS2.png/300px-AgathaJun419751745UTCSMS2.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Agatha_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Agatha_1975_track.png/150px-Agatha_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Agatha_1975_track.png/225px-Agatha_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Agatha_1975_track.png/300px-Agatha_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">June 2 – June 5</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">80 mph (130 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/>≤1005 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a>)</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>An area of disturbed weather about 290 mi (467 km) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">Acapulco</a> formed on June 1. It organized into a tropical depression the next day. After heading southwestward, it turned to the northwest and strengthened into Tropical Storm Agatha on June 2. Agatha maintained its course and steadily intensified. It reached hurricane intensity on June 3 while located about 170 mi (270 km) southwest of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zihuatanejo,_Guerrero" title="Zihuatanejo, Guerrero">Zihuatanejo</a>. Hurricane Agatha started weakening thereafter, becoming a tropical storm on June 4 and a depression on June 5. It dissipated shortly afterwards. At this time, Agatha was located about 140 mi (230 km) south of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tres_Marias_Islands" title="Tres Marias Islands">Tres Marias Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Even though Agatha passed close to Mexico as it weakened, no impact is known to have been caused.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Waves caused by Agatha did impact a ship called the <i>Polynesian Diakan</i>. A Greek freighter en route from <a href="/wiki/Pago_Pago,_American_Samoa" title="Pago Pago, American Samoa">Pago Pago</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminal_Island,_California" title="Terminal Island, California">Terminal Island, California</a>, the <i>Polynesian Diakan</i> began flooding on June 3, forcing the 32-man crew to abandon the vessel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stricken_ship_in_tow_sinks_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-stricken_ship_in_tow_sinks-6"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The exact cause of the initial flooding was unknown, but was speculated to be primarily due to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cargo_shifting&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cargo shifting (page does not exist)">cargo shifting</a> and not the weather.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-L.B._tug_races_to_get_to_freighter_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-L.B._tug_races_to_get_to_freighter-9"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The crew was rescued by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stricken_ship_in_tow_sinks_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-stricken_ship_in_tow_sinks-6"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Three members required hospitalization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-L.B._tug_races_to_get_to_freighter_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-L.B._tug_races_to_get_to_freighter-9"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> After being reached on June 6 by a tugboat in a salvage attempt, the vessel capsized and sank near <a href="/wiki/San_Clemente_Island" title="San Clemente Island">San Clemente Island</a> on June 7, taking its multimillion-dollar cargo of 71 containers of canned <a href="/wiki/Tuna" title="Tuna">tuna</a> with it. The reason for the sinking was unknown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disabled_freighter_sinks_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-disabled_freighter_sinks-7"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Tropical_Storm_Bridget">Tropical Storm Bridget</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tropical Storm Bridget"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #4DFFFF">Tropical storm (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:BridgetJun2919751645UTCSMS2.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="571" data-file-width="774" decoding="async" height="111" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/BridgetJun2919751645UTCSMS2.png/150px-BridgetJun2919751645UTCSMS2.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/BridgetJun2919751645UTCSMS2.png/225px-BridgetJun2919751645UTCSMS2.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/BridgetJun2919751645UTCSMS2.png/300px-BridgetJun2919751645UTCSMS2.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Bridget_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Bridget_1975_track.png/150px-Bridget_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Bridget_1975_track.png/225px-Bridget_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Bridget_1975_track.png/300px-Bridget_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">June 28 – July 3</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">65 mph (100 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>On June 27, a tropical depression formed about 575 mi (925 km) south of the tip of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baja_California_Peninsula" title="Baja California Peninsula">Baja California Peninsula</a> at a location atypical for <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">tropical cyclogenesis</a>. The depression moved generally westward, and intensified into Tropical Storm Bridget on June 28. It started accelerating as it turned towards the northwest, then west, and eventually to the southwest. Bridget weakened into a tropical depression on July 2 and dissipated the next day. It remnant circulation hung about in the area for a few more days. Bridget never came near land and thus, no deaths or damage were recorded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #FF9E59">Category 3 hurricane (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:CarlottaJuly519751645UTCSMS2.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="1109" data-file-width="1426" decoding="async" height="117" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/CarlottaJuly519751645UTCSMS2.png/150px-CarlottaJuly519751645UTCSMS2.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/CarlottaJuly519751645UTCSMS2.png/225px-CarlottaJuly519751645UTCSMS2.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/CarlottaJuly519751645UTCSMS2.png/300px-CarlottaJuly519751645UTCSMS2.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Carlotta_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Carlotta_1975_track.png/150px-Carlotta_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Carlotta_1975_track.png/225px-Carlotta_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Carlotta_1975_track.png/300px-Carlotta_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">July 2 – July 11</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">125 mph (205 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>A disturbance 480 mi (772 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">Acapulco</a> organized into a tropical depression on July 2. It headed generally west-northwest and steadily intensified, becoming a tropical storm later on July 2. It became a hurricane on July 3 and ultimately peaked as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 3</a> hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Hurricane Carlotta was first major hurricane, Category 3 or higher, of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> It began weakening thereafter, and was a tropical storm by July 8. Carlotta became a depression on July 10 and dissipated the next day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-8"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The hurricane did not come near land and caused no significant impact.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-9"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Hurricane_Denise">Hurricane Denise</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Hurricane Denise"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #FF738A">Category 4 hurricane (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:DeniseJuly1119751656UTC.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="1502" data-file-width="854" decoding="async" height="264" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/DeniseJuly1119751656UTC.png/150px-DeniseJuly1119751656UTC.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/DeniseJuly1119751656UTC.png/225px-DeniseJuly1119751656UTC.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/DeniseJuly1119751656UTC.png/300px-DeniseJuly1119751656UTC.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Denise_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Denise_1975_track.png/150px-Denise_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Denise_1975_track.png/225px-Denise_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Denise_1975_track.png/300px-Denise_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">July 5 – July 15</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">140 mph (220 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>An unstable area developed a circulation and became a tropical depression on July 4. It headed west- northwest and became a tropical storm on July 7. The next day, it was a hurricane and turned to the west. Continuing to strengthen, it reached Category 4 intensity on July 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-10"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> This windspeed was the highest of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The hurricane turned to the southwest and gradually weakened. By July 11 it was nearly stationary. It then headed northwestward again and continued weakening, becoming a tropical storm on July 13. It weakened into a depression on July 14 and dissipated immediately thereafter. Denise brought winds of near-<a href="/wiki/Gale" title="Gale">gale</a> force to parts of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, but otherwise caused no damage or casualties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-11"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Tropical_Storm_Eleanor">Tropical Storm Eleanor</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tropical Storm Eleanor"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #4DFFFF">Tropical storm (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:EleanorJuly1219750339UTC.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="1186" data-file-width="761" decoding="async" height="234" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/EleanorJuly1219750339UTC.png/150px-EleanorJuly1219750339UTC.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/EleanorJuly1219750339UTC.png/225px-EleanorJuly1219750339UTC.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/EleanorJuly1219750339UTC.png/300px-EleanorJuly1219750339UTC.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Eleanor_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Eleanor_1975_track.png/150px-Eleanor_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Eleanor_1975_track.png/225px-Eleanor_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Eleanor_1975_track.png/300px-Eleanor_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">July 10 – July 12</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">40 mph (65 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>An area of disturbed weather developed into a tropical depression on July 10, 100 mi (160 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">Acapulco</a>. As its <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">center of circulation</a> became more pronounced and it was upgraded into Tropical Storm Eleanor. The tropical storm moved northwestward, and later curved northward. The system made landfall near Manzanillo on July 12. It quickly dissipated. Eleanor brought 20 mph (32 km/h) winds the Manzanillo, but damage from the storm is minor due to the lack of heavy rains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-12"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Tropical_Storm_Francene">Tropical Storm Francene</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tropical Storm Francene"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #4DFFFF">Tropical storm (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Francenejul2719752345UTC.gif"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="817" data-file-width="925" decoding="async" height="132" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Francenejul2719752345UTC.gif/150px-Francenejul2719752345UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Francenejul2719752345UTC.gif/225px-Francenejul2719752345UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Francenejul2719752345UTC.gif/300px-Francenejul2719752345UTC.gif 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Francene_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Francene_1975_track.png/150px-Francene_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Francene_1975_track.png/225px-Francene_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Francene_1975_track.png/300px-Francene_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">July 27 – July 30</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">50 mph (85 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>A rapidly moving <a href="/wiki/Squall" title="Squall">squally</a> area of disturbed weather that was moving on a northwestward path was upgraded into Tropical Depression Seven on July 27. It intensified into a tropical storm later that day. Francene turned to the west and weakened into a tropical depression on July 28. It slowly weakened and ceased to exist as a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> on July 30. Francene's remnants persisted as a tropical disturbance for another twelve hours, before completely dissipating. This tropical cyclone caused no known impact.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-13"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Tropical_Storm_Georgette">Tropical Storm Georgette</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Tropical Storm Georgette"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #4DFFFF">Tropical storm (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Georgetteaug1219751915UTC.gif"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="685" data-file-width="769" decoding="async" height="134" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Georgetteaug1219751915UTC.gif/150px-Georgetteaug1219751915UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Georgetteaug1219751915UTC.gif/225px-Georgetteaug1219751915UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Georgetteaug1219751915UTC.gif/300px-Georgetteaug1219751915UTC.gif 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Georgette_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Georgette_1975_track.png/150px-Georgette_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Georgette_1975_track.png/225px-Georgette_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Georgette_1975_track.png/300px-Georgette_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">August 11 – August 14</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">45 mph (75 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>An area of disturbed weather about 800 mi (1,287 km) south-southwest of <a href="/wiki/Cabo_San_Lucas" title="Cabo San Lucas">Cabo San Lucas</a> organized a tropical depression on August 11. It was upgraded to tropical storm intensity the next day. After heading generally westward and peaking as a weak to moderate tropical storm on August 12, Georgette slowly weakened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-14"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> It weakened into a depression on August 14 and underwent <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical transition</a> thereafter. The cyclone dissipated twelve hours later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Tropical Storm Georgette had no impact on land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-15"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Tropical_Storm_Hilary">Tropical Storm Hilary</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Tropical Storm Hilary"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #4DFFFF">Tropical storm (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Hilaryaug1419752345UTC.gif"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="679" data-file-width="601" decoding="async" height="169" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Hilaryaug1419752345UTC.gif/150px-Hilaryaug1419752345UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Hilaryaug1419752345UTC.gif/225px-Hilaryaug1419752345UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Hilaryaug1419752345UTC.gif/300px-Hilaryaug1419752345UTC.gif 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Hilary_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Hilary_1975_track.png/150px-Hilary_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Hilary_1975_track.png/225px-Hilary_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Hilary_1975_track.png/300px-Hilary_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">August 13 – August 17</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">60 mph (95 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>A tropical disturbance formed on August 11 and gradually organized. The next day, an <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">atmospheric circulation</a> developed. Based on this, this system was classified as Tropical Depression Nine on August 13. The depression quickly intensified into a tropical storm and was named <i>Hilary</i>. The system gradually accelerated to the northwest<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-16"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> and reached its peak strength on August 15 with winds of 60 mph (95 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Tropical Storm Hilary then started a gradual weakening trend. It became a depression on August 16, turned a little bit more westward, and dissipated on August 17. Hilary never came near land, and as such caused no death or damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-17"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Hurricane_Ilsa">Hurricane Ilsa</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Hurricane Ilsa"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #FFD98C">Category 2 hurricane (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Ilsaaug2419752145UTC.gif"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="1017" data-file-width="1625" decoding="async" height="94" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Ilsaaug2419752145UTC.gif/150px-Ilsaaug2419752145UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Ilsaaug2419752145UTC.gif/225px-Ilsaaug2419752145UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Ilsaaug2419752145UTC.gif/300px-Ilsaaug2419752145UTC.gif 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Ilsa_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Ilsa_1975_track.png/150px-Ilsa_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Ilsa_1975_track.png/225px-Ilsa_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Ilsa_1975_track.png/300px-Ilsa_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">August 18 – August 26</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">105 mph (165 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>On August 18, a tropical depression formed south of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Tehuantepec" title="Gulf of Tehuantepec">Gulf of Tehuantepec</a> from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> that had entered the Pacific Ocean from the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. It strengthened into a tropical storm later that day. The tropical storm moved westward, and became a hurricane on August 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-18"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Ilsa became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 2 hurricane</a> on August 22. It maintained that intensity for three days as it moved out to sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> On August 25, Ilsa turned westward and rapidly weakened. It fell to tropical storm intensity on August 26. Later that day, while still maintaining winds of <a href="/wiki/Gale" title="Gale">gale</a>-force, Ilsa ceased being a tropical cyclone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Ilsa's remnants continued out over the open <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-19"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The remains of Ilsa eventually interacted with another weather system. That triggered the development of the unnamed hurricane of August 31–September 5<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC1975_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-CPHC1975-5"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Hurricane Ilsa caused no casualties or damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-20"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> As the unnamed hurricane formed from Ilsa's remnants,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC1975_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-CPHC1975-5"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Ilsa and the latter system are unofficially the same tropical cyclone. Indeed, this year's seasonal report from the <a href="/wiki/Redwood_City,_California" title="Redwood City, California">Redwood City</a> <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Pacific_Hurricane_Center" title="Eastern Pacific Hurricane Center">Eastern Pacific Hurricane Center</a>, in the <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Weather_Review" title="Monthly Weather Review">Monthly Weather Review</a></i> includes a brief discussion of the unnamed hurricane in the report's section on Hurricane Ilsa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-21"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Neither the <a href="/wiki/Central_Pacific_Hurricane_Center" title="Central Pacific Hurricane Center">Central Pacific Hurricane Center</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHCArchive_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-CPHCArchive-10"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> nor the official <a href="/wiki/HURDAT" title="HURDAT">HURDAT</a> "best track" database of tropical cyclone location and intensity data explicitly considers them the same. Hence, as far as official records matter, Ilsa and the unnamed hurricane of August 31 – September 5 are different tropical cyclones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC1975_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-CPHC1975-5"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Hurricane_Jewel">Hurricane Jewel</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Hurricane Jewel"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #FFFFD9">Category 1 hurricane (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Jewelaug2719751845UTC.gif"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="777" data-file-width="805" decoding="async" height="145" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Jewelaug2719751845UTC.gif/150px-Jewelaug2719751845UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Jewelaug2719751845UTC.gif/225px-Jewelaug2719751845UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Jewelaug2719751845UTC.gif/300px-Jewelaug2719751845UTC.gif 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Jewel_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Jewel_1975_track.png/150px-Jewel_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Jewel_1975_track.png/225px-Jewel_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Jewel_1975_track.png/300px-Jewel_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">August 24 – August 31</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">75 mph (120 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>On August 24, a tropical depression formed from a tropical disturbance about 250 mi (400 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">Acapulco</a>. It intensified into Tropical Storm Jewel the next day. The <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> headed generally northwest and paralleled the coast of Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-22"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> On August 27, Jewel became a hurricane for mere six hours,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> the minimum possible time in <a href="/wiki/HURDAT" title="HURDAT">HURDAT</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_format_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT_format-11"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Jewel then turned to the west, and then back to the northwest. Jewel weakened to depression strength on August 31. Tropical Depression Jewel dissipated shortly after that, although its remnants remained distinct until September 3. There was no impact.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-23"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Hurricane_Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Hurricane Katrina"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #FF738A">Category 4 hurricane (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Katrinasep319751545UTC.gif"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="1509" data-file-width="1573" decoding="async" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Katrinasep319751545UTC.gif/150px-Katrinasep319751545UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Katrinasep319751545UTC.gif/225px-Katrinasep319751545UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Katrinasep319751545UTC.gif/300px-Katrinasep319751545UTC.gif 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Katrina_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Katrina_1975_track.png/150px-Katrina_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Katrina_1975_track.png/225px-Katrina_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Katrina_1975_track.png/300px-Katrina_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">August 29 – September 7</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">130 mph (215 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>On August 29, an area of weather developed directly into a tropical storm, skipping the tropical depression stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Katrina steadily intensified as it headed west-northwest, gradually turning to the northwest. It peaked in intensity on September 3 as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 4</a> hurricane with winds of 130 mph (209 km/h), the second highest of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-11"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Katrina then headed westward and rapidly weakened. It was downgraded into a tropical storm on September 5 and a depression on September 6. The last advisory was issued on September 7 when Katrina dissipated. Although the hurricane brought Category 3-equivalent winds to <a href="/wiki/Socorro_Island" title="Socorro Island">Socorro Island</a> on September 2, no damage was reported there or anywhere else.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-24"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Unnamed_hurricane">Unnamed hurricane</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Unnamed hurricane"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #FFFFD9">Category 1 hurricane (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Unnamed_Hurricane_(1975).PNG"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="3216" data-file-width="2936" decoding="async" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Unnamed_Hurricane_%281975%29.PNG/150px-Unnamed_Hurricane_%281975%29.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Unnamed_Hurricane_%281975%29.PNG/225px-Unnamed_Hurricane_%281975%29.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Unnamed_Hurricane_%281975%29.PNG/300px-Unnamed_Hurricane_%281975%29.PNG 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:1975_Pacific_hurricane_12_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="2240" data-file-width="3000" decoding="async" height="112" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/1975_Pacific_hurricane_12_track.png/150px-1975_Pacific_hurricane_12_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/1975_Pacific_hurricane_12_track.png/225px-1975_Pacific_hurricane_12_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/1975_Pacific_hurricane_12_track.png/300px-1975_Pacific_hurricane_12_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">August 31 – September 5</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">75 mph (120 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/>990 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a>)</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The remnants of Hurricane Ilsa interacted with a mid-level trough to produce a new low-level circulation on August 31, about 930 miles (1,500 km) northeast of Hawaii. The new system absorbed the remnants of Ilsa as it moved westward. Thunderstorms increased around the low, resembling a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical cyclone</a> by September 1. Its track over warmer waters caused the storm to transition into a tropical storm on September 2, by which time the convection organized into rainbands. By that time, the storm was about 685 miles (1,102 km) north of the Hawaiian island of <a href="/wiki/Kauai" title="Kauai">Kauai</a>. The storm quickly developed a <a href="/wiki/Central_dense_overcast" title="Central dense overcast">central dense overcast</a>, and by 00:00 UTC on September 3 <a href="/wiki/Dvorak_technique" title="Dvorak technique">Dvorak classifications</a> began on the cyclone. An approaching cold front turned the storm to the north and northeast. Around that time, the storm developed an eye in the center of the convection, suggesting that it intensified into a hurricane late on September 3, located about 1,170 miles (1,885 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>. It was a small system, measuring about 85 miles (140 km) in diameter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-potm_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-potm-12"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> A nearby ship reported sustained winds of 63 mph (102 km/h), while another nearby ship reported a pressure of 1003 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (29.6 <a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">inHg</a>), with a 3-hour tendency increase of 13.5 mbar (0.40 inHg), suggesting a minimum pressure of under 990 mbar (29 inHg).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-25"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-potm_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-potm-12"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unu_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-unu-13"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> After maintaining hurricane status for about 18 hours, the storm weakened due to strong wind shear from the approaching cold front. Early on September 5, the cyclone became <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Alaska" title="Gulf of Alaska">Gulf of Alaska</a>, while located about 315 miles (510 km) southwest of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juneau" title="Juneau">Juneau</a>, Alaska. On the same day, the circulation was rapidly absorbed by the frontal system,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unu_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-unu-13"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> and the remnants quickly reached the coast of <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC1975_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-CPHC1975-5"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-potm_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-potm-12"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The remnants of the storm turned southeastward, and the low was last tracked definitively to a point north of <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unu_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-unu-13"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Hurricane_Lily">Hurricane Lily</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Hurricane Lily"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #FFFFD9">Category 1 hurricane (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Lilysep1819752215UTC.gif"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="1186" data-file-width="1306" decoding="async" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Lilysep1819752215UTC.gif/150px-Lilysep1819752215UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Lilysep1819752215UTC.gif/225px-Lilysep1819752215UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Lilysep1819752215UTC.gif/300px-Lilysep1819752215UTC.gif 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Lily_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Lily_1975_track.png/150px-Lily_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Lily_1975_track.png/225px-Lily_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Lily_1975_track.png/300px-Lily_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">September 16 – September 21</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">85 mph (140 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Rain and wind reports from the <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">Acapulco</a> weather station suggested that a tropical depression was developing. This was confirmed on September 16 from ship reports. It intensified into a tropical storm and was named Lily. Tropical Storm Lily headed northwest in a nearly straight path. By 0000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a> September 18, the system was a moderate tropical storm located about 160 mi (260 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Manzanillo,_Colima" title="Manzanillo, Colima">Manzanillo</a> Lily was upgraded to hurricane status on the same day and eventually reached its peak strength. After passing southwest of <a href="/wiki/Socorro_Island" title="Socorro Island">Socorro Island</a>, Lily weakened, becoming a storm on September 20. Lily weakened into a depression on September 21, turned to the west-southwest, and dissipated. Its remnants remained visible until September 24, when they were no longer distinct via <a href="/wiki/Weather_satellite" title="Weather satellite">weather satellite</a>. On September 19, the cyclone brought hurricane-force winds to Socorro Island. No damage or casualties were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-26"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Tropical_Storm_Monica">Tropical Storm Monica</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Tropical Storm Monica"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #4DFFFF">Tropical storm (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Monicasep2919751845UTC.gif"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="591" data-file-width="842" decoding="async" height="105" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Monicasep2919751845UTC.gif/150px-Monicasep2919751845UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Monicasep2919751845UTC.gif/225px-Monicasep2919751845UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Monicasep2919751845UTC.gif/300px-Monicasep2919751845UTC.gif 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Monica_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Monica_1975_track.png/150px-Monica_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Monica_1975_track.png/225px-Monica_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Monica_1975_track.png/300px-Monica_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">September 28 – October 2</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">50 mph (85 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>In late September, three tropical disturbances moved westward across the eastern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. One of them organized into a tropical depression on September 28. The next day the depression was upgraded to Tropical Storm Monica. The tropical storm initially headed west-northwestward and gradually curved towards the north. It reached its peak intensity of 50 mph (85 km/h) on September 29. Monica then gradually weakened. It became a tropical depression on October 1. The cyclone dissipated shortly thereafter. No impact was reported in association with Tropical Storm Monica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-27"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Tropical_Storm_Nanette">Tropical Storm Nanette</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Tropical Storm Nanette"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #4DFFFF">Tropical storm (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Nanettesep2919751845UTC.gif"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="653" data-file-width="979" decoding="async" height="100" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Nanettesep2919751845UTC.gif/150px-Nanettesep2919751845UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Nanettesep2919751845UTC.gif/225px-Nanettesep2919751845UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Nanettesep2919751845UTC.gif/300px-Nanettesep2919751845UTC.gif 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Nanette_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Nanette_1975_track.png/150px-Nanette_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Nanette_1975_track.png/225px-Nanette_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Nanette_1975_track.png/300px-Nanette_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">September 28 – October 4</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">50 mph (85 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>On September 28, a system developed into a tropical depression, simultaneously with Tropical Storm Monica.. It headed generally westward and on September 29, 18 hours after Monica did it, strengthened into a tropical storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-12"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-28"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Nanette continued out to sea and slowly strengthened. It peaked in windspeed of 50 mph (85 km/h) on October 3 and turned to the southwest. It then slowly weakened. On October 4, it weakened into a tropical depression and then degenerated into a disturbance that same day. No damage or deaths were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-29"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Hurricane_Olivia">Hurricane Olivia</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Hurricane Olivia"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #FF9E59">Category 3 hurricane (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Oliviaoct2419752215UTC.gif"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="1788" data-file-width="1787" decoding="async" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Oliviaoct2419752215UTC.gif/150px-Oliviaoct2419752215UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Oliviaoct2419752215UTC.gif/225px-Oliviaoct2419752215UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Oliviaoct2419752215UTC.gif/300px-Oliviaoct2419752215UTC.gif 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Olivia_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Olivia_1975_track.png/150px-Olivia_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Olivia_1975_track.png/225px-Olivia_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Olivia_1975_track.png/300px-Olivia_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">October 22 – October 25</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">115 mph (185 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Olivia_(1975)" title="Hurricane Olivia (1975)">Hurricane Olivia (1975)</a></div>
<p>On October 22, Tropical Depression Eighteen formed from a cluster of three thunderstorms south of Mexico and then strengthened into a tropical storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-30"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> It moved northwestward initially, followed by a northeast turn. Olivia then intensified steadily. It reached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 3</a> intensity and a peak of 115 mph (185 km/h) winds just before landfall. Early on October 25, Olivia moved ashore just south of <a href="/wiki/Mazatl%C3%A1n" title="Mazatlán">Mazatlán</a> causing major damage. Olivia destroyed 7,000 houses in the region,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-31"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> leaving 30,000 people homeless,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap1026_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap1026-14"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> and damage totaled $20 million (1975 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>, $113 million 2024 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-32"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The hurricane killed 30 people,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-calgary_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-calgary-15"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> 20 of them were from drownings in shrimp boats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-33"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Tropical_Storm_Priscilla">Tropical Storm Priscilla</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Tropical Storm Priscilla"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="infobox" style="width: 24em;"><tbody><tr><td class="infobox-subheader" colspan="2" style="font-weight: bold; background: #4DFFFF">Tropical storm (SSHWS)</td></tr><tr><td class="infobox-full-data" colspan="2"><div style="vertical-align: middle"><span class="nowrap"><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Priscillanov519751545UTC.gif"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="1585" data-file-width="1629" decoding="async" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Priscillanov519751545UTC.gif/150px-Priscillanov519751545UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Priscillanov519751545UTC.gif/225px-Priscillanov519751545UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Priscillanov519751545UTC.gif/300px-Priscillanov519751545UTC.gif 2x" width="150"/></a></span><span class="nowrap"> </span><span typeof="mw:File"><a class="mw-file-description" href="/wiki/File:Priscilla_1975_track.png"><img class="mw-file-element" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Priscilla_1975_track.png/150px-Priscilla_1975_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Priscilla_1975_track.png/225px-Priscilla_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Priscilla_1975_track.png/300px-Priscilla_1975_track.png 2x" width="150"/></a></span></span></div></td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Duration</th><td class="infobox-data">November 2 – November 7</td></tr><tr><th class="infobox-label" scope="row">Peak intensity</th><td class="infobox-data">65 mph (100 km/h) <small>(1-min)</small>;<br/></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>A nearly stationary cloudy area developed a <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">circulation</a> on November 2. The depression drifted north for a while before turning to the west-northwest on November 3. It intensified into a tropical storm on November 4. Priscilla then began turning to the north. However, the system failed to re-curve, instead, the tropical storm turned generally westward. The tropical storm peaked in windspeed as a 65 mph (100 km/h) high-end storm on November 5. It then turned to the northwest on a heading nearly directly at <a href="/wiki/Clarion_Island" title="Clarion Island">Clarion Island</a>, but it dissipated on November 7<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-best_track_2-13"><a href="#cite_note-best_track-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> about 115 mi (185 km) short of landfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-34"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="" style="clear:both;"></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Other_systems">Other systems</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Pacific_hurricane_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Other systems"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Besides the tropical storms and hurricanes this season, there were four additional tropical depressions within the eastern Pacific. None of them made landfall anywhere or crossed into the central Pacific. Tropical Cyclone Four formed on July 2 and dissipated the next day. Tropical Cyclone Thirteen formed on September 12 and lasted four days. Tropical Cyclone Seventeen formed October 16 and dissipated a day later. Tropical Cyclone Nineteen formed on November 1 and also dissipated the next day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-35"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>
